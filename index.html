About Me

I am a systems programmer with knowledge in and experience with low-level systems development, the Linux programming interface, and the programming languages Rust, C++, and C. Broadly, my interests include operating systems and systems programming, software development and testing, software profiling and performance, and Rust programming, which I do extensively in my dissertation research. I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Pennsylvania. My advisor is Dr. Joseph Devietti.

Research Interests

I love systems programming. I am very familiar with the Linux programming interface and system call API. Also, I am a huge fan and avid user of the Rust programming language, and I also have experience programming with C/C++.

My doctoral research uses low-level systems techniques such as system call interception and manipulation to provide reproducibility and performance optimizations for unmodified Linux programs. My goal is to make systems that improve existing code by providing safety guarantees and/or performance improvements without extra effort on the part of the programmer.
